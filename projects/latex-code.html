LaTeX Formula Code Generation from Images

Taneem Ullah Jan

University of Engineering and Technology Peshawar, Pakistan
Personal Research Project

*Work done after Bachelor Thesis

Abstract

In recent years, there has been increasing interest in developing automated systems that can convert images of mathematical formulas to LaTeX code. Such systems have the potential to greatly assist individuals who frequently use LaTeX, such as researchers and students. However, this task poses the challenges that require both computer vision and natural language processing techniques, and lies at the intersection of both. This is a similar task to the problem of image captioning. In this paper, we present a dual setting encoder–decoder model that utilizes a soft attention mechanism to address the task of converting images of mathematical formulas to LaTeX code. Our model is trained on a relatively large dataset of 100,000 LaTeX formulas scraped from ArXiv. We also evaluate the performance of our model using several metrics, including perplexity, exact match, BLEU score, and Levenshtein distance, to assess both the code–level and image–level accuracy of the generated LaTeX code.

These experimental results demonstrate that our model achieves a BLEU score of 78% and an image edit–distance of 62%, indicating its high performance in converting images of mathematical formulas to accurate LaTeX code. Our proposed approach offers a promising solution to the challenging task of Image–to–LaTeX conversion and has the potential to significantly improve the efficiency and accuracy of LaTeX–based workflows in academic and scientific settings.
